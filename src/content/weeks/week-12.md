## The week in one sentence

This is it — you plan, build, and ship one complete project of your own, then launch a
portfolio that puts everything you've made on the internet under your name.

## What we're really learning

Capstone week isn't about new syntax; it's about **putting it all together** like a real
developer: scope an idea, wireframe it, build a semantic structure, style it with Tailwind,
make it interactive with Alpine, feed it live data with `fetch`, then **polish and ship**.
That full loop — from blank repo to a live URL you're proud of — is the skill employers and
clients actually care about.

Not sure what to build? Pick from the **capstone menu**:

- A **personal portfolio** with a contact section.
- A **small-business landing page** with one interactive feature.
- A **dashboard** — recipe, movie, or weather — driven by a public API.
- An **event page** with an RSVP form and a live attendee list.

## Your pro-skills, all at once

This week leans hard on **Ship + SEO**, **accessibility**, and **performance**. Run
Lighthouse and act on it: convert images to WebP/AVIF, and never lazy-load your hero image
(it's the first thing people see). Do a real accessibility pass, and add SEO and Open Graph
tags so your link previews nicely when shared.

> Be proud: you can now take an idea and put a fast, accessible, interactive site online by
> yourself. That's a genuine, durable skill. 🎓

This is **foundational front-end** — strong and real, and also a launch pad. The natural
next step is frameworks like React and a backend, when you're ready. Today, though: you're
a web developer with a portfolio to prove it. Congratulations.
